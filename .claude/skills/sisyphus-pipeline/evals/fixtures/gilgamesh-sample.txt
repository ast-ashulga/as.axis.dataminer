Tablet I: He Who Saw the Deep

He who saw the Deep, the country's foundation,
who knew the proper ways, was wise in all matters.
Gilgamesh, who saw the Deep, the country's foundation,
who knew the proper ways, was wise in all things.

He it was who crossed the ocean, the wide seas, to the rising sun,
who explored the world regions, seeking life.
It was he who restored the holy places that the Flood had destroyed,
and set in place for the people the rites of the cosmos.

Two-thirds of him was god, one-third of him human.
The Lady of the Gods drew the form of his figure,
while his build was perfected by divine Nudimmud.

Surpassing all kings, for his stature renowned,
heroic offspring of Uruk, a charging wild bull.
He walks out in front, the leader, and walks at the rear, the trust of his brothers.

The people of Uruk grew troubled in their hearts:
Gilgamesh leaves no son to his father, day and night his arrogance grows.
The men of the city muttered against him; the women cried out to the goddess.

Tablet II: The Coming of Enkidu

Aruru washed her hands, she pinched off some clay, she threw it into the wild.
In the wild she created Enkidu, the hero, offspring of silence, knit strong by Ninurta.
His whole body was shaggy with hair, he was furnished with tresses like a woman.

He knew neither people nor country; he was dressed as cattle are.
With the gazelles he grazes on grasses, with the herd he drinks at the waterhole,
with the beasts his heart delights at the water.

A hunter, a trapper-man, came face to face with him by the waterhole.
The hunter saw him and his expression froze, and he went back to his house in fear.
